## COMMON ISSUES & SOLUTIONS

### Issue: "The research is too generic"
**Solution**: Provide more specific research questions in Section D. Instead of "What's their AI strategy?", ask "What specific AI use cases has their CEO mentioned in recent earnings calls?"

### Issue: "The conversation scripts sound robotic"
**Solution**: Practice them out loud and rewrite in your own voice. The scripts are frameworks, not word-for-word recitations.

### Issue: "I can't find information on specific people"
**Solution**: Try alternate spellings, search LinkedIn specifically, look for conference speaker bios, check company press releases for executive quotes.

### Issue: "The package is overwhelming"
**Solution**: Start with just 3 documents: `00_START_HERE.md`, `14_cheat_sheet.md`, and `18_executive_brief.md`. That's 80% of the value.

### Issue: "Information contradicts between sources"
**Solution**: Flag it in the research notes, present both perspectives, and note which source is more credible/recent.

## PRO TIPS

### Research Phase:
1. **Search for direct quotes** - Leadership quotes are gold for understanding priorities
2. **Check recent (last 30 days) news** - Fresh hooks for conversation
3. **Look at job postings** - Reveals strategic priorities and tech stack
4. **Find competitor customer wins** - Shows what they value
5. **Track conference appearances** - Where to intersect naturally

### Conversation Engineering:
1. **Practice out loud** - Scripts should sound natural, not memorized
2. **Prepare 3 versions** of your pitch - Technical, business value, analogical
3. **Listen for verbal cues** - Their language reveals priorities
4. **Have an "out"** - Graceful exits if not interested
5. **Make it a conversation** - Ask as much as you tell

### Strategic Positioning:
1. **Define your category** - Don't let them put you in wrong box
2. **Know your "unlike"** - "Unlike X, we Y"
3. **Prepare for "so what?"** - Every claim needs a "so what?" answer
4. **Have proof points** - Specific examples, not vague claims
5. **Make it about them** - Their problems, their goals, their success

### Follow-up:
1. **Send within 24 hours** - Strike while iron is hot
2. **Reference specific conversation points** - Shows you listened
3. **Provide value first** - Don't immediately ask for next step
4. **Multi-touch without being annoying** - Vary your approach
5. **Track everything** - CRM or spreadsheet for follow-up cadence

2. **Verify critical facts** - Don't trust single sources
3. **Look for primary sources** - Company statements > news articles > rumors
4. **Note information date** - 2025 news > 2023 news
5. **Flag assumptions clearly** - "Likely..." vs. "Confirmed..."

### Preparation Phase:
1. **Read out loud** - Scripts should sound natural
2. **Practice with partner** - Role-play scenarios
3. **Customize, don't memorize** - Use frameworks, not scripts
4. **Prepare questions** - Conversation, not monologue
5. **Physical prep** - Business cards, materials, appearance

### Engagement Phase:
1. **Listen more than talk** - 70/30 rule
2. **Reference research naturally** - Don't be creepy
3. **Read the room** - Adjust on the fly
4. **Take brief notes** - Follow-up reference
5. **Focus on relationship** - Not just transaction

### Follow-up Phase:
1. **Send within 24 hours** - Strike while hot
2. **Reference conversation** - Show you listened
3. **Provide value first** - Don't immediately ask
4. **Multi-touch strategy** - Persistence, not annoying
5. **Track everything** - What works, what doesn't
